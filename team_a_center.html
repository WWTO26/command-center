Optimization or fixed margin — is your mode right for this event?
Getting inventory and moving it too fast, getting inventory but not enough volume, or stuck being the highest bidder without actually landing inventory → consider fixed margin. Inside ~3 hours of the event on lower-priced inventory → consider optimization.
Decision required per event
↳ Tap for full framework

How each mode works

  • Optimization: set a min margin (e.g. 15%) and a max margin (always 100%). The system bids to be highest in the market, but never below your min margin and never above your max margin. Not getting good offers? Raise your min margin (and min profit floor, if you use one) so the system won't settle for less.
  • Fixed margin: bid a flat margin regardless of what anyone else is bidding. Use this when you want to maximize inventory acquired at a margin you're comfortable with, without chasing the market.

Turn optimization OFF when...

  • Volume play: you're getting inventory easily at a high margin (e.g. 35%+) and moving it too fast. Switch to a flat margin to chase volume instead of just staying on top of the market.
  • Getting shut out: you're the highest bidder but still not landing inventory. Drop to a lower flat margin.
  • High-margin trap: you're the highest at a high margin, but not getting inventory.
    • Don't default straight to a low fixed margin.
    • Example: 5 hours before an event, listings are thin, and optimization shows you're the highest at 90%. Don't cut straight to 15%.
    • Check what optimization is actually landing at across your zones — say it's landing at 60% to stay highest.
    • Use roughly half of that (or a bit more, or a bit less) as your fixed margin benchmark — e.g. 30% — not the full 60%, and not your 15% min margin.

Turn optimization ON when...

  • Inside roughly 3 hours of the event, on lower-priced inventory (below ~$100).
    • People are more willing to sell to you regardless of what you're offering — this is your chance to actually steal inventory cheap.
    • Best for average, not-too-competitive shows where you're not chasing volume — just need to be the highest when it counts.

DIMR — inventory & pricing review
The value in DIMR is seeing how stale listings are and when they were last touched. The goal: keep inventory moving while getting good yield.
—
Step 1 — Load your saved view
Open the saved view filtered to Gray (Team A). This is your working list for the whole DIMR pass.
Step 2 — Listings at Floor
Go through everything sitting at floor price — that means we're overpriced. Fix the pricing on each one before moving on.
Step 3 — Last Criteria Update: find what's stale
Sort by Last Criteria Update (and Last Sale where useful). A listing doesn't need to be at floor to need attention — if it's stale and not moving, take a second look. What counts as stale depends on how far out the event is:
Event timingLast criteria updateAction
~1 week out3+ days without an updateReconsider pricing now
Months outUpdated a few days agoNo action needed yet
Any timingNo sale in ~3 weeksCheck it — ask why it isn't moving
Any timingNo sale in ~1 monthAlways reconsider pricing
Already undercut and not selling? Check your margin, not just the calendar — this overrides the table above.
  • Margin above 30%: you have room — undercut further or lower the price more to actually land the sale.
  • Margin below 20%: leave it — no cushion left to give up.
  • Margin 20–30%: judgment call for now.
